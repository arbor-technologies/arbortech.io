A few things you might be wondering.

Arbor is a strategic alignment platform. In plain language: it connects what your company wants to achieve (goals, OKRs, initiatives) to what your team actually does every day (tasks, projects, deliverables) in one hierarchy that flows downward from strategy, not upward as status reports. Think of it as the layer between "here's our vision" and "here's my to-do list" that most companies are currently managing with spreadsheets, meetings, and wishful thinking.

Because the early adopters shape what gets built. Your problems, your workflows, your "I wish it did this" moments go directly into the product. You also lock in early adopter pricing permanently. And the teams who join early tend to get the most value, because the product is literally built around their reality.
